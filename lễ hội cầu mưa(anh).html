The festival to pray for rain is held from April 6 to 8 every year, at Phap Van pagoda, Lac Hong commune, Van Lam district. The festival of praying for rain is associated with the system of worshiping the Four Dharmas and praying for the nation's peace and harmony, so that the people can have a bountiful harvest and live a happy and prosperous life. Phap Van Pagoda (also known as Thai Lac) is an ancient temple dating back to the Ly dynasty, located in the temple complex of Tu Phap (Phap Van, Phap Vu, Phap Loi and Phap Dien). Legend has it that, every time there is a drought, the people here hold a ceremony to pray for rain; so the festival is not held regularly every year. Until 2005, the festival of praying for rain really became the happiest festival of the people here. On this day, in addition to the religious meaning of worshiping the Four Dharmas (Phap Van, Phap Vu, Phap Loi, Phap Dien) in the Northern Delta, it is also an opportunity for families to gather, discuss business, and develop products. production, breeding, plowing… Four Dharmas is a noun that refers to four goddesses in Vietnamese beliefs including: Clouds, Rain, Thunder and Lightning, representing natural phenomena that play an important role in agricultural society. According to scientists, the Four Dharmas beliefs were shaped and developed during the early introduction of Buddhism into our country, demonstrating a sense of independence and self-control in preserving national cultural identity and spiritual connection. the spirit of solidarity of the whole people in opposing the assimilation of feudalism in the North. Belief in worshiping the Four Dharmas is a phenomenon of indigenous beliefs in our country with bold colors of wet rice civilization combined with Buddhism. Rice farmers always need water. Among the four essential needs that Vietnamese farmers have summarized as "water, manure, seed, seed", water is the first. But in this tropical monsoon region, talking about water is talking about rain. The Tu Phap pagoda festivals show us vividly that the union between Buddhism and the beliefs of Vietnamese farmers still taking place… According to tradition, at Phap Van Pagoda, during a drought, people bring Phap Van statue out of the temple, along with statues of Phap Loi and Phap Vu from nearby pagodas to On Xa pagoda (known as Un pagoda) ) - the place to worship Lady Phap Dien to pray for rain. The procession takes place as follows: at the forefront of the procession is the dragon dance team. Next are the participants in the procession, they are healthy people, topless, wearing loincloths. Especially in the festival, it is indispensable to have a musical ensemble. People standing on both sides of the road waiting for the procession to pass, splashing water, doing so to pray for good luck, those who get caught in water will be lucky. Let's all get through the palanquin together, wishing for health and peace According to the legend of the people, Mrs. Phap Dien can only stay in the temple and cannot go out, because if she goes outside the temple door, there will be a drought. The team to process Mrs. Phap Dien just ran out to the temple door to greet the sisters (Phap Van, Phap Vu, Phap Loi) and then came back in. Like the festivals of agricultural residents, the festival of praying for rain is a form of community cultural activities associated with the spiritual life of farmers. As a festival originating from agriculture and serving agricultural purposes, in the minds of farmers, opening a festival to pray for rain is as necessary and important as any production stage.
